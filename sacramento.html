City of Sacramento Website

Sacramento, capital of the U.S. state of California, lies at the confluence of the Sacramento River and American River. The district of Old Sacramento harkens back to the city’s Gold Rush era, with wooden sidewalks and wagon rides. One of several museums in Old Sacramento, the California State Railroad Museum depicts the construction of the Transcontinental Railroad, one of the country’s earliest technological feats.

Fun Facts

  • Sacramento was founded on February 27, 1850.
  • Popular attractions in Sacramento include the California State Capitol, Old Sacramento, Crocker Art Museum, and the Sacramento Zoo.
  • Sacramento played a crucial role during the California Gold Rush of the mid-1800s. It served as a major distribution point for gold seekers heading to the Sierra Nevada mountains in search of their fortunes.
  • Sacramento is often referred to as the “City of Trees” due to its abundant tree-lined streets, parks, and green spaces.
  • Old Sacramento, with its preserved historic buildings and cobblestone streets, offers a glimpse into the city’s past and is a popular tourist destination.
